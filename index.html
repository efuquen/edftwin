My work at Google.

Some Spider

Head of Infrastructure & Platform Engineering

October 2014 - October 2015

Manage the AWS infrastructure for SomeSpider, which include digital magazine publications such as Cafe, The Mid, and Scarymommy; serving tens of millions of uniques a month. Manage CI tools and deployments to CoreOS clusters (consisting of a few dozen machines), using docker containers to keep a consistent environment. Server management done in Ansible and with a number of op tools written in Bash, Python, and Go. In charge of maintaining and developing our backend platforms, including restful APIs in Node and a number of backend services in Node and Python that interact with the other micro services via RabbitMQ

Bloomberg

Technical Team Lead - Finance/Market Web Services

March 2014 - October 2014

Lead a team of engineers to develop and maintain web based services to financial data from Terminal backed services for both internal (Bloomberg.com, Bloomberg Mobile, BLAW, BGOV, etc.) and external clients. These services managed tens of millions of requests a day and delivered consistent response times in the 10's of ms. Technologies developed in Scala, Java, NodeJS, and Python while integrating with multiple data stores (MySQL, memcached, elasticsearch).

Getty Images

Independent Contractor

June 2013 - March 2014

Worked with a team of a dozen or so software engineers to kickstart a new project in Scala. This project's goal was to become the new backbone for Getty Image asset ingestion (images, video, and audio) and provide a modern and consistent api and pipeline of those ingested images throughout the business. I provided guidance on good software development and design, particularly on the Scala programming language and tooling. I leveraged my previous work in scalable high performance systems to lead the development of the REST based API's to this new system, using Akka and Spray. I also worked heavily in developing the video transcoding piece using Rhozet, but exploring FFmpeg as a possible alternative. I designed the system with future requirements and growth in mind to lay the foundation for further work in this area.

ScriptEd

Volunteer

November 2014 - Present

ScriptEd is an non-profit organization that provides students at under-resourced schools with access to fundamental coding skills. Along with three other volunteers, I teach HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and JQuery at a local high school. Twice a year we hold hackathons as mid-terms and finals to help cement the materials they have learned for the semester, during this time I help mentor students as they work.
